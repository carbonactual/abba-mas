# Universal Participation and Inclusion Doctrine

Carbon Actual is designed to receive and serve people, enterprises, organizations, governments, associations, clubs, communities, institutions, products, and machines through flexible and appropriate entry routes.

## Core position

The ecosystem is open to everyone.

Participation must not be denied or reduced because of:

- sex or gender
- sexual orientation
- age
- disability
- race or ethnicity
- religion or belief
- nationality or language
- income or social class
- education level
- relationship status
- political affiliation
- geography
- digital literacy
- organizational size or legal form

Male and female. Gay and straight. Young and old. Individual and institution. Local and global. Digital and non-digital.

## Different beings, different routes

Equality does not mean forcing everyone through the same interface, process, or expectation.

The ecosystem must provide different entry and participation routes for:

- individual humans
- children and young people with guardian protections
- adults
- older persons
- persons with disabilities
- families and households
- informal groups
- clubs and associations
- cooperatives
- schools and universities
- companies and enterprises
- charities and foundations
- professional bodies
- traditional institutions
- governments and public agencies
- international organizations
- AI agents, devices, and products under accountable human authority

## Entity model

Every participant enters as an entity with an appropriate profile:

- identity or recognized representation
- role and authority
- needs and goals
- capabilities
- consent status
- rights and responsibilities
- accessibility needs
- language and communication preferences
- risk and safeguarding requirements
- products, services, assets, and liabilities where applicable
- history and proofs

An entity may represent one person, many people, a legal body, an informal community, a government department, a product, or a machine-controlled service.

## Flexible onboarding

Onboarding must adapt to the participant rather than demanding that the participant adapt completely to the system.

Supported routes should include:

- mobile and web
- chat and voice
- assisted human onboarding
- organization-admin onboarding
- delegated and representative onboarding
- guardian-supported onboarding
- low-bandwidth and offline-assisted routes
- document and API integration
- physical service points
- batch and institutional import

## Accessibility by default

Every public product should be designed for:

- clear language
- readable interfaces
- screen-reader compatibility
- keyboard navigation
- captions and transcripts
- adjustable text and contrast
- low-bandwidth operation
- multilingual support
- voice and assisted interaction
- simple and advanced modes

Accessibility is not a special product. It is a standard operating requirement.

## Identity without forced exposure

Participants should be able to prove what is necessary without exposing everything about themselves.

The ecosystem must separate:

- legal identity
- public identity
- private identity
- role identity
- organizational identity
- pseudonymous participation where lawful

Sensitive personal characteristics must not be publicly exposed or used for ranking unless the participant explicitly authorizes a valid and necessary use.

## Safeguarding and age

Young people, older persons, vulnerable persons, and persons under guardianship require stronger protections, not reduced dignity.

The ecosystem must support:

- guardian and representative authority
- age-appropriate interfaces
- safeguarding escalation
- limited data exposure
- clear redress
- revocation and correction
- human review for consequential decisions

## Organizational flexibility

Organizations may enter at different maturity levels:

- informal group
- registered association
- club
- cooperative
- small enterprise
- large enterprise
- nonprofit
- professional body
- educational institution
- government body
- multinational organization

The ecosystem should not require enterprise-grade complexity from a small group or force a government to operate like an individual account.

## Equal opportunity and contextual fairness

Every participant must have a fair chance to access opportunity, demonstrate value, receive service, and improve.

Fairness must account for context. Equal treatment may require different support, interfaces, time, language, evidence routes, or safeguarding.

Ranking and access must not be influenced by protected personal characteristics. They must be based on relevant, consented, and provable factors.

## Respect and conduct

Every participant must be treated with dignity.

The ecosystem does not require agreement on identity, lifestyle, belief, politics, or culture as a condition for participation. It requires lawful conduct, respect for others, truthful representation, consent, and compliance with shared boundaries.

Harassment, dehumanization, exclusion, exploitation, and targeted abuse are incompatible with participation.

## Human final authority

AI may assist, classify, recommend, translate, automate, and optimize. It must not become the final authority over a person's dignity, identity, consent, or irreversible personal consequence.

Human appeal and review must remain available for consequential decisions.

## Product requirement

Every product brief must declare:

- who can participate
- who may be unintentionally excluded
- required accessibility routes
- language support
- age and safeguarding requirements
- individual and organizational onboarding paths
- public and private identity boundaries
- human review and redress

A product is incomplete if its design excludes lawful participants without a necessary and documented reason.

## Final rule

Open to everyone.
Flexible by context.
Equal in dignity.
Protected by consent.
Accessible by design.
Accountable in consequence.
