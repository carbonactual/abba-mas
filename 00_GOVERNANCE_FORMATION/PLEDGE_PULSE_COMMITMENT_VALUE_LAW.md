# Pledge Pulse and Commitment Value Law

Pledge is PULSE.

Commitment is VALUE.

This is the clean distinction.

## Core meaning

A pledge is the signal that something has been declared, promised, intended, assigned, expected, or offered.

Because pledge is signal, pledge is PULSE.

Commitment is the value behind the pledge.

Because commitment is dedicated value, commitment is VALUE.

## Flow

Pledge is made.
PULSE is released.
Commitment is measured.
VALUE is read.
Action happens.
Return PULSE comes back.
Commitment value updates.

## Example

A person pledges to attend a wedding.
The pledge is PULSE.

The person gives time, transport, presence, clothing, appearance, social proof, and network participation.
That dedication is commitment.
The commitment is VALUE.

## Service example

A person pledges to serve food.
The pledge is PULSE.

The person arrives, serves, helps the event move, supports the family, and reduces burden.
That commitment is VALUE.

## Vendor example

A person pledges to buy brocade or perfume from a vendor.
The pledge is PULSE.

The purchase, appearance, vendor earnings, social presentation, and event readiness are commitment VALUE.

## Failed pledge

If a pledge fails, it is still PULSE.

The failed pledge may carry warning, delay, liability, learning, reputation change, or recovery route.

The commitment value may be absent, reduced, converted, deferred, or damaged.

## Final law

Pledge is PULSE.
Commitment is VALUE.
Pledge signals intention.
Commitment carries dedication.
Pledge starts the route.
Commitment gives weight to the route.
Return PULSE proves what happened.
