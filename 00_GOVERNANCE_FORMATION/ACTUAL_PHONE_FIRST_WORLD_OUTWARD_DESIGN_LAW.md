# Actual Phone-First, World-Outward Design Law

The phone is the primary access point to Actual because most signals, communication, coordination, location, relationships, services, and daily activity pass through it.

But the phone is not the destination of Actual.

Actual must use the phone to return the human to life outside the screen.

## Core direction

Phone-first does not mean phone-bound.

The phone should:

- receive and organize signals
- connect the human and personal AI
- surface relevant Becoming
- coordinate people, places, time, and movement
- support safety and continuity
- help the human act in the physical and social world

The phone should not become a redundant container where every part of life is repeated in one place.

## Distance principle

The farther the human moves from unnecessary phone dependence, the better Actual is working.

Distance from the phone may mean:

- physical distance
- less screen attention
- fewer repeated interactions
- more direct human contact
- more movement through places
- more embodied activity
- more outdoor and social participation
- more trust in the personal AI to coordinate quietly in the background

Actual should not reward keeping the human inside the interface.

Actual should reward successful release from the interface into real life.

## World-outward design

Actual should encourage:

- adventure
- outdoor activity
- movement
- exploration
- travel
- physical presence
- human connection
- local discovery
- community participation
- real-world work
- play
- nature
- shared experiences

The measure of success is not screen time.
It is useful movement, better connection, lived experience, and human growth.

## Distributed experience

Becoming may begin on the phone, but it should continue through:

- locations
- people
- events
- groups
- physical environments
- devices
- services
- institutions
- transport
- workspaces
- homes
- nature

The phone coordinates the experience.
The world completes it.

## Non-redundancy rule

Information should appear where it is most useful.

Actual must not duplicate the same feed, action, or information across every surface without purpose.

Each surface should carry only what fits its context.

Examples:

- location guidance appears when movement is relevant
- friendship curation appears within relationship context
- outdoor activity appears when time, safety, weather, and location align
- group action appears inside the group environment
- public identity appears through ATLAS
- deep personal context remains in Root

## Anti-capture rule

Actual must not be designed to trap attention.

The interface should reduce friction, then release the human back into action.

The best interaction may be brief:

see
→ understand
→ move
→ experience
→ return PULSE

## Success measure

A strong Actual experience should create:

- less unnecessary screen time
- fewer repeated taps
- faster movement into the world
- more meaningful human contact
- more outdoor and physical participation
- more completed commitments
- richer return PULSE
- stronger Root, Continuum, merit, connection, and starmap formation

The interface should disappear once its work is done.

## Final law

The phone is the main doorway into Actual.

It is not the whole of Actual.

Actual must use the phone to coordinate life, then help the human move outward into people, places, adventure, nature, work, and shared experience.

The farther the human can move from unnecessary phone dependence while remaining safely connected, the better Actual is working.

The phone starts the movement.
The world is where Actual happens.
