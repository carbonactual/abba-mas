## Mathematical functions as operating laws

### BODMAS — order of operation
- Brackets: boundaries, scope, consent and data limits
- Orders: authority, priority, proof level and sequence
- Division: allocation of work, budget, risk and capability fractions
- Multiplication: scale, replication and network effects
- Addition: aggregation of apps, teams, signals and value
- Subtraction: correction, pruning, waste removal and risk reduction

No bracket, no operation.

### Binary — first decision
Binary logic creates the first branch: eligible or ineligible, safe or unsafe, active or paused, proven or unproven. Binary starts routing but does not reduce complex reality to only two states.

### Integration — formation
Integration combines app fractions, swarms, humans, AI, data, delivery routes and proofs into a product engine.

### Differentiation — contribution
Differentiation separates each app's true marginal contribution from the total team outcome. It identifies which fraction improved speed, quality, cost, proof, growth or resilience.

### Quadratic — interaction effects
Quadratic logic models non-linear outcomes where two or more capabilities create more or less value together than they do separately. It is used to detect compatibility, interference and compounding.

### Prime numbers — irreducible capability
Prime logic identifies capability fractions that cannot be decomposed further without losing their function. Prime nodes may become specialist, foundational or protected resources.

### Fibonacci — staged growth
Fibonacci logic governs organic expansion, test allocation, retry growth, team scaling and pathway development. Growth increases by evidence and prior formation rather than uncontrolled duplication.

### Pi and circularity — return and continuity
Pi governs cycles, radius, coverage, recurrence and the return of apps from teams to their swarms. A completed task closes one circle while opening a larger one.

### Radius and diameter — reach and span
Radius measures how far one app or swarm can affect the ecosystem from its center. Diameter measures the full span across connected pathways.

### Angles — direction and alignment
Angles measure directional fit between an app, team, product goal, human need and ecosystem pathway. Acute alignment is direct; wider angles indicate indirect routes, friction or redirection.

### Pythagorean relation — balanced formation
Pythagorean logic tests whether combined dimensions such as capability and delivery can support the required outcome without hidden imbalance.

### Complex and imaginary numbers — latent possibility
Real numbers represent observed performance. Imaginary components represent unproven, simulated, future or latent capability. Complex values hold both together without confusing possibility with proof.

### Even and odd — symmetry and exception
Even logic supports pairing, balance and mirrored comparison. Odd logic preserves the unmatched case, exception, edge condition or decisive differentiator.

### Greater than, less than and equal to — ranking
These operators compare outcomes while equality identifies equivalent performance under the same constraints.

### Mean, median and mode — fair evidence
Mean measures average performance, median resists extreme distortion, and mode identifies the most common outcome. Rankings must use all three where relevant.

### Harmonic measures — rate balance
Harmonic logic compares rates such as latency, throughput, cost per task and reciprocal performance without allowing one extreme metric to dominate unfairly.

### Fractal logic — repeated structure
The same laws repeat at app, fraction, swarm, team, product and ecosystem levels. Each layer is smaller or larger but structurally related.

### Infinity-to-infinity — continuous Becoming
Every proven result creates further branches, combinations, risks, opportunities and returns. ABBA does not attempt to execute infinite possibilities at once. It compresses them into bounded, ranked and testable pathways.

## League application

Each app receives fair trials. Each fraction is measured independently and in teams. Teams compete under equal constraints. Results are differentiated by contribution, integrated into ecosystem learning, and returned to the home swarm.

The goal is not a permanent winner. The goal is continuous discovery of the strongest formation for each context, followed by optimization, rechallenge and growth.
