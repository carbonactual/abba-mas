# I/O Trading and CAPITAL Architecture

## Canonical placement

Trading is a foundational domain of **I/O**, the Carbon Actual movement layer.

- **Phase 1 — Ecosystem Trading:** internal and partner exchange across Carbon Actual.
- **Phase 2 — Product and HAPI Trading:** reusable trading embedded in every product and consent-governed HAPI route.
- **Phase 3 — CAPITAL:** the dedicated universal trading product.
- **Phase 4 — Trading-as-a-Service:** external APIs, SDKs, white-label markets and managed market operations.

- Credit, financing, insurance and risk transfer

## CAPITAL domains

CAPITAL supports lawful market objects across forex, securities, commodities, food, livestock, metals, minerals, energy, recyclables, reusables, by-products, carbon credits, offsets, environmental units, crypto, DeFi, futures, derivatives, assets, liabilities, property, infrastructure, AI, compute, data, capabilities, ideas, intellectual property, rights, contracts, opportunities and future markets.

## Product inheritance

Every ecosystem product receives the same trading primitives through domain adapters. SPARE can trade parts and repair services; Property can handle sales, leases and facility capacity; Institute GPT can exchange learning and certification services; Management can offer outsourced operational capability. Product teams own domain rules, while I/O owns movement and settlement.

## HAPI authority

HAPI agents may observe, compare, recommend, prepare, request quotations and negotiate within explicit mandates. Sensitive, regulated, high-value or irreversible trades require human approval through SEAL.

## Market boundaries

Every object must pass legality, ownership, transferability, consent, ethics, risk, compliance, valuation, settlement and proof gates. Human organs are never commercial market objects. Human capabilities are exchanged only as consented services or availability. Regulated financial execution requires licensed providers and jurisdiction-aware controls.
