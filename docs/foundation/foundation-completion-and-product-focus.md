# Foundation Completion and Product Focus

## Decision

Carbon Actual is one ecosystem product with a complete shared operating foundation and specialized product branches.

The foundation is now treated as architecturally complete enough for concentrated product development. New product requirements must first map to an existing universal layer. A new universal layer may be created only when the capability is genuinely required across multiple unrelated products and cannot be represented as a policy, service, object or workflow inside an existing layer.

## Complete universal layer model

### 1. Identity and subject layer
Persistent # identities for humans, AIs, entities, roles, products, assets, places, projects, documents and market objects.

### 2. Root and continuity layer
Foundational truth, provenance, history, memory, inheritance, records and long-term continuity.

### 3. Direction and capability layer
VECTOR, goals, desired state, constraints, milestones, eligibility, competence, capacity and unlocks.

### 4. Relationship, commitment and allocation layer
Relationships, authority, appointments, obligations, contracts, tasks, resources, permissions and responsibility.

### 5. Governance, policy and jurisdiction layer
Constitution, doctrine, laws, policies, decision rights, regulatory context, legal entities, jurisdiction and dispute authority.

### 6. SEAL, trust, safety and privacy layer
Consent, access, confidentiality, safeguarding, moderation, fraud controls, identity assurance, suitability, revocation and protected actions.

### 7. Data, knowledge and intelligence layer
Index, Root, structured data, documents, semantic memory, research, knowledge graphs, analytics, models, forecasts and explainable intelligence.

### 8. ATLAS, time and environment layer
Location, maps, boundaries, infrastructure, physical context, timelines, calendars, temporal validity, environmental conditions and future scenarios.

### 9. Communication and experience layer
Messaging, calls, notifications, inbox, feeds, collaboration, language, accessibility, localization, interfaces and omnichannel delivery.

### 10. Discovery and market layer
Search, recommendation, matching, directories, listings, demand requests, marketplaces, reputation and opportunity curation.

### 11. Management and orchestration layer
Governance, strategy, operations, projects, people, assets, finance, risk, workflows, agents, swarms and Management-as-a-Service.

### 12. I/O and value layer
Accounts, wallets, ledger, payments, credit, escrow, collateral, fees, taxes, settlement, value routing, rewards and liabilities.

### 13. Trading and exchange layer
Offers, bids, negotiation, barter, auctions, swaps, orders, markets, delivery and CAPITAL specialist infrastructure.

### 14. Pulse, Proof and Actual layer
Events, evidence, verification, state transitions, outcomes, provenance, audit and achieved value.

### 15. Lifecycle and continuity layer
Creation, onboarding, activation, operation, maintenance, transfer, inheritance, recovery, suspension, deprecation, archival and deletion.

### 16. Integration and interoperability layer
APIs, SDKs, events, webhooks, adapters, imports, exports, connectors, identity federation and provider abstraction.

### 17. Security, resilience and infrastructure layer
Authentication, authorization, encryption, key management, backups, availability, incident response, observability, deployment and disaster recovery.

### 18. Quality, assurance and improvement layer
Testing, validation, standards, certification, monitoring, feedback, experimentation, accessibility review, performance and continuous optimization.

### 19. Economics, sustainability and impact layer
Business models, pricing, incentives, contribution attribution, environmental impact, carbon, social value, unit economics and long-term viability.

### 20. Support, remedy and dispute layer
Help, cases, complaints, appeals, mediation, claims, refunds, remediation, enforcement and restoration.

## Cross-cutting laws

Every product branch must:

- inherit all applicable shared layers;
- use the same identity, consent, proof, value and audit universes;
- define its domain objects and workflows without duplicating foundation services;
- distinguish past evidence, present action and future forecast;
- distinguish concept, simulation, commitment, physical state and verified Actual;
- maintain human final authority for consequential actions;
- expose APIs and events through shared interoperability standards;
- include security, accessibility, localization, testing, support and lifecycle handling from the beginning;
- support management, communication, trading and I/O only where relevant to its domain;
- record every important state transition through Pulse, Proof and Actual.

## Foundation freeze rule

The architecture is now frozen at the layer level.

Future discoveries should normally result in one of:

1. a capability inside an existing shared layer;
2. a policy or schema addition;
3. a reusable service;
4. a product-specific module;
5. an external provider adapter.

They should not automatically become new top-level layers.

## Product concentration rule

Product work now proceeds vertically:

```text
user problem
→ complete product journey
→ domain model
→ inherited shared services
→ executable APIs and workflows
→ user interface
→ tests and security
→ sandbox deployment
→ evidence and review
→ production gate
```

The immediate focus is to finish one end-to-end usable flow at a time rather than continuously enlarging the universal architecture.
