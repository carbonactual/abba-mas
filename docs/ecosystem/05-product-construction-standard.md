# Industry Product Construction Standard

Products are built around specific industries and the entities that operate within them. A product is not only a dashboard; it is an operating, economic, evidence, intelligence and relationship environment for its entity network.

## Required product definition
Every industry product must define:
1. Industry, subsector and boundary
2. Current problems and underserved participants
3. Past records and inherited liabilities
4. Present operational model
5. Future forecasts, foresight, scenarios and frontier opportunities
6. Human, AI, organisational, physical, digital, financial, legal, operational, environmental and future entities
7. Entity relationships and authority graph
8. User roles and scoped agent roles
9. Assets, liabilities, value states and ownership
10. Workflows, cases, transactions and lifecycle states
11. Regulations, standards, controls and audit rules
12. Risk, incident, dispute, appeal and recovery processes
13. Proof conditions and Actual outcomes
14. Marketplace objects, tradables, services, procurement and fulfilment
15. Pricing, revenue, payments, settlement, insurance, credit and value routing
16. Learning, certification, onboarding and orientation through Institute GPT
17. Communications and collaboration
18. Data model, APIs, events, connectors and migration paths
19. Dashboards for HAPIs, owners, workers, providers, regulators and ecosystem operators
20. Internal management and external Management-as-a-Service offer

## Standard product family
A mature domain may provide:
- Core Industry OS
- Industry Management product
- Industry Marketplace and trading product
- Finance, credit and capital product
- Insurance and protection product
- Compliance, legal, audit and proof product
- Learning, certification and workforce product
- Data, search, analytics and intelligence product
- Asset, maintenance, repair and SPARE product
- Communications and relationship product
- Forecast, foresight, simulation and digital-twin product
- HAPI personal office for that industry

## Inheritance rule
Domain products inherit shared identity, Root, VECTOR, Index, Continuum, Vault, SEAL, I/O, communication, management, marketplace, Pulse, Proof, Actual, reputation, audit, payments, value routing and forecasting. Domain teams add only specialised entities, relationships, rules, workflows, interfaces and intelligence.

## Interoperability rule
One entity may participate in many products without duplication. The core identity and shared facts remain continuous; domain-specific facts stay in their authorised context. Cross-domain actions use explicit consent, purpose, minimum necessary data and auditable routes.

## Delivery forms
- standalone SaaS
- API or embedded module
- white-label platform
- mobile, web, field, voice, messaging, USSD or offline-first application
- co-managed or fully managed service
- fractional specialist team
- embedded management office
- outcome-based service
- ecosystem-native product

## Build path
Research → domain map → entity graph → value map → workflow map → data contract → MVP → integration adapters → proofs and controls → managed-service playbook → marketplace → forecast layer → sector expansion → frontier preparation.
