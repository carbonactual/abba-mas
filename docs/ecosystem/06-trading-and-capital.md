# Trading and Capital — Universal Ecosystem Trading Layer

Trading is a universal Carbon Actual capability. It allows authorised entities to discover, value, negotiate, exchange, allocate, transfer, finance, hedge, settle, insure, prove, and manage value across industries, time horizons, and market structures.

`Capital` is the specialised product family for trading, exchange, market infrastructure, capital formation, and future market opportunities. Inside the shared ecosystem architecture, trading connects to I/O for movement and settlement, Actual Management for governance and operations, Marketplace for discovery and fulfilment, SEAL for authority and consent, Pulse for market activity, Proof for verification, and Actual for completed outcomes.

## Canonical trading doctrine

1. Any lawful, ethically permitted, identifiable and verifiable value object may become tradable when ownership, authority, risk, transfer conditions, valuation, and Proof are defined.
2. Trading is not limited to buying and selling. It includes bidding, auction, barter, exchange, tender, negotiation, subscription, rental, lease, licensing, lending, borrowing, staking, pooling, syndication, securitisation, tokenisation, hedging, futures, options, swaps, forwards, escrow, brokerage, clearing, settlement, donation, grant, allocation, matching, procurement and reverse markets.
3. Trading may apply to present assets, future production, rights, capacity, access, claims, liabilities, obligations, services, ideas, capabilities, data, environmental value and other authorised market objects.
4. Every trade must preserve provenance, participant authority, applicable restrictions, valuation basis, risk disclosures, settlement terms, dispute routes and evidence.
5. HAPIs and authorised organisations retain final authority. Agents may search, analyse, recommend, negotiate within scope and prepare orders, but may not exceed SEAL permissions.
6. Illegal markets, coercive transfers, exploitative human-body trade, trafficking, non-consensual rights transfer and prohibited goods are excluded.

## Industry integration rule
Every relevant industry product must expose authorised tradable objects to Capital through a domain trading adapter. The industry remains responsible for specialist rules; Capital provides shared market infrastructure.

Examples:
- Agriculture exposes crops, livestock, inputs, equipment, storage, futures and by-products.
- Energy exposes generation, fuel, certificates, battery capacity, grid services and future production.
- SPARE exposes parts, replacements, repair services, recovered products and compatibility Proof.
- Property exposes sale, rent, lease, time-bound access, development rights and maintenance contracts.
- Institute GPT exposes lawful courses, certifications, mentorship, training capacity and verified skills.
- Actual Management exposes management services, fractional teams, managed operations and outcome contracts.
- AI products expose scoped models, agents, compute, capabilities and service-level commitments.

## Universal trade lifecycle
Discover → Qualify → Verify identity and authority → Define market object → Establish ownership and restrictions → Value → List or request → Match → Negotiate or bid → Risk and compliance checks → Agree contract → Fund or secure → Escrow or clearing → Deliver or perform → Inspect and verify → Settle → Record Pulse → Create Proof → Record Actual → Reputation and aftercare.

## Trade object requirements
Every trade object must define:
- identity and owner
- asset, service, right, claim, liability, capacity or future-value type
- domain and jurisdiction
- description, quantity, quality and condition
- provenance and Proof
- price or valuation method
- currency or value unit
- transferability and restrictions
- delivery, performance or access terms
- risk, insurance and compliance requirements
- settlement, escrow and dispute terms
- taxes, fees and value routing
- expiry, lifecycle and future obligations

## Safety and governance
- KYC/KYB and authority checks proportionate to risk
- AML, sanctions, fraud and market-abuse controls where applicable
- suitability and risk disclosures for complex financial instruments
- custody segregation and auditable settlement
- conflict-of-interest and insider-information controls
- price manipulation, wash trading and collusion monitoring
- consumer protection, refunds, warranties and dispute resolution
- prohibited-market screening
- human welfare, dignity, environmental and future-generation safeguards
- regulatory adapters by jurisdiction and asset class

## Temporal trading scope
- Past: provenance, historical prices, ownership, obligations, dormant assets, claims, failed trades, restitution, legacy instruments and forecast accuracy.
- Present: listings, orders, bids, negotiations, delivery, settlement, custody, risk and market operations.
- Future: forwards, futures, options, pre-orders, future production, capacity reservations, scenario markets, climate positions, AI capabilities, space resources and frontier opportunities.
