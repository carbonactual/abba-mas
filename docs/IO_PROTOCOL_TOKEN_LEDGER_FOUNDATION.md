# I/O Protocol, Token, Ledger, Mint and Exchange Foundation

I/O is the common financial and value foundation for every Carbon Actual product, HAPI, agent, machine, institution, marketplace and future research domain.

## Communication and transaction routes

I/O supports typed routes for:

- HAPI to HAPI
- agent to HAPI
- HAPI to agent
- agent to agent
- person to person
- person to agent
- agent to person
- business to agent
- agent to business
- government to agent
- agent to government
- machine to machine
- robot to HAPI
- HAPI to robot
- product to product
- institution to institution

Every route requires identity scope, SEAL consent, permissions, purpose, value truth state, policy decision, audit trail, settlement boundary and revocation.

## Protocol foundation

Supported protocol families include MCP, A2A, x402, AP2, HTTP APIs, OpenAPI, GraphQL, JSON-RPC, gRPC, WebSockets, webhooks, event streams, Matrix, Nostr, DIDComm, ActivityPub, secure messaging, payment requests, QR, NFC, USSD and delay-tolerant messaging.

Protocol presence never grants authority. Each protocol adapter is disabled by default and receives only the minimum capability required.

- payment tokens
- settlement tokens
- internal credits
- utility tokens
- access tokens
- governance signals without automatic authority
- loyalty and reward records
- asset-reference tokens
- rights tokens
- licence tokens
- contribution tokens
- proof tokens
- receipt tokens
- identity-bound records
- non-transferable records
- environmental claims

Each token records issuer, owner or controller, rights, restrictions, backing, redemption, transferability, jurisdiction, evidence, expiry, truth state and settlement status.

## NFT and mint foundation

Canonical assets are minted once. Later interactions create usage, licence, transfer, royalty or contribution receipts rather than duplicate ownership.

NFT ownership does not automatically grant copyright, physical ownership, commercial rights, revenue rights, training rights or control of an underlying asset. Rights must be explicit.

Minting supports media, prompts, workflows, datasets, models, software, certificates, tickets, products, agents, robots, machines, physical assets, spare parts, property rights, licences and BE collectibles.

## Open-ledger architecture

I/O uses layered ledgers:

1. Private Vault for identity, credentials and sensitive agreements.
2. Authorized I/O double-entry ledger for balances, obligations, settlements and treasury.
3. PULSE resource ledger for consumed and created value.
4. PROOF ledger for verified outcomes and commitments.
5. Public ATLAS only for consented public proofs and aggregate records.
6. Optional public-chain anchoring for selected commitments.

Open ledger must never become open surveillance.

## P2P, trade and barter

I/O supports direct and mediated exchange of money, goods, services, access, rights, time, skills, capacity, credits and verified value. Routes include direct exchange, escrow, atomic or coordinated swap, staged exchange, pooled purchase, cooperative settlement, barter balancing, invoice netting, multilateral clearing and marketplace matching.

Every exchange exposes valuation assumptions, fees, delivery obligations, evidence, dispute routes, legal status, tax context and final settlement state.

## DeFi and decentralization

DeFi capabilities remain modular and disabled by default. Research and sandbox scope includes swaps, liquidity routing, lending, borrowing, staking, collateral, insurance, bridges, derivatives, stablecoins, account abstraction and decentralized identity.

Every protocol requires chain, contract addresses, audit status, exploit history, oracle dependence, liquidity, governance, concentration, bridge, liquidation and jurisdiction risk records.

## Environment and chain selection

No blockchain is labelled environmentally harmless. Chain selection considers energy model, validator concentration, hardware requirements, transaction capacity, settlement finality, data availability, fees, bridge dependence, public transparency, privacy and fit for purpose.

Polygon and other proof-of-stake networks may be evaluated for lower operational energy than proof-of-work alternatives, but all environmental claims remain evidence-based and time-stamped.

## Robotic and humanoid finance

I/O supports bounded finance for robots, humanoids, drones, machines and autonomous fleets:

- identity and owner/operator scope
- maintenance reserves
- energy and charging
- parts and SPARE integration
- insurance and liability
- work receipts
- machine earnings
- operator earnings
- depreciation and wear
- compute and connectivity
- task escrow
- human override
- emergency shutdown costs
- leasing and usage finance

Machines do not receive unrestricted custody, signing, borrowing or ownership powers by default.

## SPARE finance

SPARE consumes I/O for repair finance, parts credit, warranty support, replacement finance, vendor settlement, escrow, compatibility evidence, product-life valuation, insurance, lease-to-own and recovery of residual value.

## Foundation law

All money, tokens, NFTs, mints, credits, legal-tender interfaces, P2P exchanges, DeFi positions, robot earnings and speculative value objects remain separate by type, truth state, rights, liquidity and settlement status.
